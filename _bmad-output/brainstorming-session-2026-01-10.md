## The Toys (Satisfying Micro-Actions)

### Stocking Shelves - Satisfying Micro-Action
- Transform chaos ‚Üí perfect order
- All labels facing outward
- Progressive audio feedback: pitch rises as shelf fills (1-9), ka-ching on 10/10!
- Visual feedback: shelf brightens, labels snap straight, sparkle on complete

### Other Satisfying Actions
- Pulling fresh famichiki from the fryer (crispy sound!)
- Tidying bathroom and back room
- Setting up seasonal displays
- Door ding-dong when customers enter
- Register ka-ching

## Session Summary

### Most Promising Concept

**Dunkin' - Cozy Konbini Simulation**

A browser-based, keyboard-only cozy simulation where you run a Japanese convenience store. The game focuses on the zen satisfaction of small tasks (stocking shelves, tidying) balanced with warm social connections with regular customers.

**Why This Stands Out:**
- Unique setting with strong aesthetic identity (konbini culture)
- Clear "toy" mechanics discovered (shelf stocking with audio feedback)
- Well-defined emotional target (chill, decompression, competence)
- Scope fits browser platform perfectly
- Dual gameplay modes create variety without stress

### Key Insights

1. **The core "toy" is transformation** - chaos to order, empty to full, messy to clean
2. **Audio feedback is crucial** - progressive pitch rising creates anticipation and satisfaction
3. **Anti-stress is a feature** - deliberately NOT mirroring real konbini pressure
4. **Dual modes prevent monotony** - Zen solo tasks + Social customer moments
5. **Japanese authenticity matters** - famichiki, takkyubin, matsuri - real konbini details

### Recommended Next Steps

1. **Prototype the shelf-stocking mechanic** - Test the audio feedback system
2. **Create a Game Brief** - Formalize the vision, scope, and pillars
3. **Define 3-5 regular customer characters** - Start building the social layer
